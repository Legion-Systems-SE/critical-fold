## Abstract

Rotating projections of a zeta-zero-seeded 3D node cloud reveal **eight
grid-invariant frequencies**: {1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 15, 27, 45}, generated by
exactly three primes {3, 5, 7}. These frequencies separate into a
face-axis set {1, 3, 5, 7} (square wave harmonics) and a diagonal
extension {9, 15, 27, 45}. Including the phase sign of each frequency
component collapses both channels to a single generator: powers of 2.

The second-difference tension of physical constants' digit sequences
factors through the same structural primes {2, 3, 5, 7}. Constants whose
tension stays within these primes to full known precision include the
speed of light, the fine structure constant, and the first two Riemann
zeta zeros. Constants that break this quantization do so exclusively
with primes 11 or 13 — the first two primes beyond the structural set.

All findings are reproducible from the included scripts and engine runs.

## Finding 1: Eight Grid-Invariant Frequencies

**Method:** Rotate the engine's node cloud (registry.npy) around four
axes (X, Y, Z, [1,1,1] diagonal) at 720 angles. Project to 2D. Measure
density variance at each angle. FFT the variance signal.

**Result:** Eight frequencies appear at all three grid sizes tested
(45, 65, 89). Frequencies that appear at one grid size but not others
are resolution artifacts; these eight are structural.

| Frequency | Factorization | Set |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| 1 | 1 | face |
| 3 | 3 | face |
| 5 | 5 | face |
| 7 | 7 | face |
| 9 | 3^2 | diagonal |
| 15 | 3 x 5 | diagonal |
| 27 | 3^3 | diagonal |
| 45 | 3^2 x 5 | diagonal |

Three prime generators: {3, 5, 7}. Prime 7 appears only in the face
set. The diagonal set is built exclusively from {3, 5}.

**Reproduce:**
```
python3 manifold_sim/engine_emergent.py --bifurcation zeta --auto --grid 45
python3 manifold_sim/engine_emergent.py --bifurcation zeta --auto --grid 65
python3 manifold_sim/engine_emergent.py --bifurcation zeta --auto --grid 89
```

## Finding 2: Face/Diagonal Asymmetry

**Result:** Face axes distribute power equally between the face and
diagonal frequency sets (squareness = 0.50, locked across all grids).
The [1,1,1] diagonal concentrates power in the face set, and this
concentration increases with grid resolution:

| Grid | Face axis squareness | Diagonal squareness |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|
| 45 | 0.501 | 0.587 |
| 65 | 0.501 | 0.806 |
| 89 | 0.500 | 0.908 |

The diagonal is the more "square-wave" axis, not the face. The face
axes pair their frequencies into four coherent doublets at two amplitude
levels: (1,9), (7,15) at high amplitude; (3,27), (5,45) at low.

## Finding 4: The Phase Sign Resolves Both Channels

**The error:** Initial analysis used unsigned amplitudes, treating all
frequency components as positive. This produced second-difference
tensions containing prime 11 in the diagonal channel.

**The correction:** The FFT phase encodes sign. Diagonal frequencies
at phase near +/-pi are inverted — they carry a factor of -1.

| Channel | Unsigned amplitudes (x3) | Signed amplitudes (x3) |
|---------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| Face | [3, 1, 1, 3, 3, 3, 1, 1] | [3, 1, 1, 3, 3, 3, 1, 1] |
| Diagonal | [3, 1, 3, 3, 1, 1, 1, 1] | [-3, -1, 3, 3, -1, -1, -1, -1] |

Second-difference tension:

| Channel | Unsigned Delta-2 | Signed Delta-2 |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Face | [2, 2, -2, 0, -2, 2] | [2, 2, -2, 0, -2, 2] |
| Diagonal | [20, -11, -11, 11, 0, 0] | [2, -4, -4, 4, 0, 0] |

With the phase sign carried, **both channels resolve to powers of 2**.
The prime 11 was an artifact of unsigned measurement. The structural
generator of both channels is a single prime: 2.

## Finding 6: Prime Quantization of Physical Constants

**Method:** Encode each constant's significant digits, compute the
second-difference sequence (Delta-2), factor each value. Check whether
all prime factors lie within the structural set {2, 3, 5, 7}.

**Result at 9 significant digits:**

| Constant | Depth | Total | Status | Breaking prime |
|----------|-------|-------|--------|---------------|
| c (speed of light) | 7 | 7 | fully resolved | -- |
| alpha (fine structure) | 7 | 7 | fully resolved | -- |
| z1 (1st zeta zero) | 7 | 7 | fully resolved | -- |
| z2 (2nd zeta zero) | 7 | 7 | fully resolved | -- |
| G (gravitational) | 7 | 7 | fully resolved | -- |
| Na (Avogadro) | 5 | 7 | breaks at [5] | {11} |
| pi | 4 | 7 | breaks at [4] | {11} |
| z3 (3rd zeta zero) | 4 | 7 | breaks at [4] | {11} |
| h (Planck) | 3 | 7 | breaks at [3] | {13} |
| phi (golden ratio) | 3 | 7 | breaks at [3] | {11} |
| k (Boltzmann) | 1 | 7 | breaks at [1] | {13} |
| e (elem. charge) | 0 | 7 | breaks at [0] | {11} |
| euler (Euler's e) | 0 | 7 | breaks at [0] | {11} |

The breaking primes are exclusively {11, 13} — the first two primes
beyond the structural set. No constant requires 17 or any larger prime
to first break quantization.

**Extended precision:**

| Constant | Digits tested | Depth before break | Status |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|--------|
| alpha | 17 | 17 | **still fully resolved** |
| z1 | 30 | 19 | breaks at pos 17 with {11} |
| z2 | 30 | 29 | breaks at pos 27 with {11} |
| pi | 30 | 4 | localized defect at [4,5] |
| euler | 30 | 0 | saturated with {11, 13} |

The fine structure constant alpha remains fully resolved at maximum
known measurement precision (17 significant digits). The first zeta
zero z1 resolves to 19 digits; z2 to 29 digits.

**Statistical note:** For uniformly random digits 0-9, the probability
of a single Delta-2 value needing a prime > 7 is 6.8%. The probability
of all values passing at 9 digits is ~61%, so individual full resolution
is not significant. However, Euler's e having 7 breaks in 28 positions
(expected: 1.9, z-score: 3.8, p < 0.001) is statistically significant
as an outlier.

## Falsifiable Predictions

1. **Alpha holds.** If the fine structure constant alpha is measured to
   20+ significant digits, its digit tension should remain within
   {2, 3, 5, 7}. If a prime > 7 appears, this prediction fails.

2. **The eight frequencies are topological.** Testing on non-cubic
   lattices (FCC, BCC, hexagonal) should either reproduce the same
   eight frequencies (structural claim) or produce different frequencies
   (cubic-specific claim). Both outcomes are informative.

3. **The -1 carry generalizes.** Other spectral analyses of the engine
   that show prime-11 or prime-13 artifacts should resolve to structural
   primes when phase signs are correctly propagated.

4. **Frequency 7 remains phase-invariant.** At higher grid resolutions
   (grid 121, 181), the phase shift at frequency 7 across the
   face-to-diagonal transition should remain near zero.
